## Team Task Breakouts

### Seedling Team (Karmen + Carl)

**Team size:** ~20 people

This team handles all transplanting and direct seeding. We'll use the bed-spacing line tool for consistent plant placement.

#### Bed Preparation (Before Planting)

Each bed must be prepared before transplanting or seeding:

1. **Weed the bed** - Remove all weeds, roots and all
2. **Level and rake** - Smooth the soil surface
3. **Check/replace drip lines** - Replace and attach drip lines, make sure functional
4. **For direct sow beds only:**
   - Clear all mulch from bed surface
   - Rake to fine, level tilth
   - Set up sprinkler system (3 rotating sprayers per 80 ft row, covering 2-3 rows)
   - Connect sprinklers to hoses

#### Planting Patterns

We use a 30" × 30" grid (bed width), subdivided into 4×4 squares of 7.5" each. The bed-spacing line marks this grid.

**Edge inset rule:** All patterns inset plants ~3.75" from bed edges to protect from trampling. This means:

- Pattern 4 (between-grid) is our maximum density - plants are centered in squares, naturally inset
- Patterns 3, 2, and 5-star also inset from edges by ~3.75"

#### Transplant Spacing Guide

Use the bed-spacing line to mark plant positions. Space transplants according to this guide:

| Crop | Variety | Pattern | Spacing | Row Ft | Notes |
|------|---------|---------|---------|--------|-------|
| Broccoli | Belstar F1 | 2 | 30" | 36 | Large single heads |
| Broccoli | Happy Rich F1 | 3 | 15" | 24 | Sprouting type, multi-harvest |
| Cabbage | Bronco F1 | 2 | 30" | 36 | Heads need space |
| Cauliflower | Tessaury F1 | 2 | 30" | 36 | Large plants |
| Chinese Cabbage | Bilko F1 | 2 | 30" | 36 | Upright, 18-30" recommended |
| Pak Choi | Black Summer F1 | 3 | 15" | 32 | Compact, 12-18" recommended |
| Lettuce (Single) | Sunland | 5-star | ~15" | 39 | 10-12" recommended |
| Beets | Boldor | 3x5 | 10" × 7.5" | 12 | 3 rows, 7.5" in-row |
| Beets | Boro F1 | 3x5 | 10" × 7.5" | 12 | 3 rows, 7.5" in-row |
| Kale | Black Magic | 5-star | ~15" | 85 | 128 plants ordered separately |

**Broccoli note:** Belstar is a standard heading type (single large head harvest) - needs full 30" spacing. Happy Rich is a sprouting type that produces smaller florets continuously over weeks - can be planted closer at 15" (pattern 3) for higher yield.

**Beets note:** These are mature transplants (no thinning needed). Use 3x5 row pattern: 3 rows across the bed (10" apart), 7.5" spacing in-row. This gives ~4" clearance for 3"+ diameter beets.

**Transplanting tips:**

- Dig hole slightly larger than root ball
- Plant at same depth as in cell tray (or slightly deeper for leggy seedlings)
- Firm soil gently around roots
- Water immediately after transplanting

#### Direct Sow Spacing Guide

**Seeder row configurations** (3 passes across 30" bed):

- **3×4 = 12 rows** @ ~2.5" spacing (intensive baby crops)
- **3×2 = 6 rows** @ ~5" spacing (carrots, radishes, spinach)
- **3×1 = 3 rows** @ ~10" spacing (full-size greens)

#### Scatter Method (Baby Greens)

For arugula, greens mix, and baby lettuce:

1. Rake bed to fine, level tilth
2. Broadcast seed evenly across bed surface
3. Rake lightly to cover seeds (1/16" - 1/8" depth)
4. Tamp gently with back of rake or board
5. Water with fine mist - keep consistently moist

#### Using the Johnny's Seeds Pinpoint Seeder

The 4-row pinpoint seeder plants rows at 2¼" apart. For a 30" bed, make 3 passes:

**Row configurations:**

- Use all 4 hoppers: 3×4 = 12 rows @ ~2.5" (intensive)
- Use 2 hoppers: 3×2 = 6 rows @ ~5" (standard root crops)
- Use 1 hopper: 3×1 = 3 rows @ ~10" (full-size greens)

**Operating steps:**

1. **Fill hopper(s)** with seeds (one variety at a time)
2. **Select seed plate** - Match hole size to seed:
   - Small holes: Carrots, spinach
   - Medium holes: Radishes, mustard, chicory
3. **Set furrow depth** - Adjust opener to match planting depth
4. **Make 3 passes** - Walk at steady pace, align wheels with previous pass marks
5. **Cover seeds** - Drag chain covers lightly; rake larger seeds if needed
6. **Mark rows** - Use stakes/flags to identify what was planted

**Tips:**

- Practice on a short section first
- Keep hoppers from running empty (causes gaps)
- Clean between varieties to prevent mixing
- For very small seeds, mix with sand for visibility

#### Irrigation Setup for Direct Sow Beds

- **3 rotating sprayers per 80 ft row**
- Sprayers should cover 2-3 adjacent rows
- Connect to main hose line
- Test water pressure and coverage before leaving bed
- Direct sow seeds need consistent moisture for germination - sprinklers should run 2-3x daily for first 2 weeks

### Mulch Team (Dave + AWU Volunteer)

**Team size:** 15-40 people (depending on turnout)

This team handles all mulching across the farm. Target depth is 4" everywhere (some areas may need more).

#### Mulching Priorities

Work in this order:

1. **Prep/Organizing** - Collect tools, review systems, orient to mulch piles, remove any debris/objects in paths (garden hoses, etc.)
2. **Garden beds** - Start with plastic-covered beds first (see below), then transplant beds
3. **Aisles** - All pathways between beds
4. **Inner farm borders** - Edges inside the main garden area
5. **Seating areas** - Around benches and gathering spots
6. **Toilet area** - Humanure station surroundings
7. **Outer edges** - Starting with west side (cover existing cardboard)

#### Before Mulching

**Plastic-covered beds - handle first:**

1. Remove plastic carefully (avoid tearing)
2. Fold neatly
3. Store under/near solar panels (shaded area keeps plastic from degrading)

**Remove palm seedlings!** Palm seedlings must be weeded out before mulching - they'll push right through mulch if just covered.

- Use a shovel to dig out roots completely
- Get ALL roots - they'll regrow if left behind
- Compost pulled seedlings

#### Mulching Guidelines

- **Target depth:** 4" minimum everywhere
- **Around plants:** Keep mulch 2-3" away from plant stems (prevents rot)
- **On cardboard:** Apply extra depth (5-6") to hold cardboard down and speed decomposition
- **Aisles:** Can go thicker (4-6") for durability

#### Associated Task: West Side Cleanup

Before mulching the west side border, this area needs clearing:

1. **Remove all tools** - Return to tool area or shed
2. **Remove palettes** - Stack near firewood area or dispose
3. **Remove debris and trash** - Bag trash, compost organic debris
4. **Relocate drip lines** - Move any drip lines back inside garden beds where they belong

#### Workflow

1. **Start with beds** - Split team: some weed palm seedlings ahead, others follow with mulch
2. **Wheelbarrow brigade** - Keep steady flow of mulch from pile to work areas
3. **Spread and level** - Use rakes to spread evenly to 4" depth
4. **Move outward** - After beds, work through aisles → borders → seating → edges
5. **West side last** - Clean up debris first, then mulch over cardboard

#### Follow-on Task: Compost

Once Sisters Garden area has been weeded, add weeds to compost pile. Dave manages the compost - he'll direct placement and layering.

- All pulled weeds → compost pile
- Organic debris from cleanup → compost pile
- Compostable plates from meal → compost pile (NOT trash)

### Sisters Garden Team (Michael D + Plant-Knowledgeable Volunteer)

**Team size:** 10-15 people

This team cleans and preps the Sisters Garden area. **Critical: Do not remove plants we want to keep!**

#### Plants to KEEP

These plants must NOT be removed:

- **Garlic** - Green shoots, papery bulbs underground
- **Edible chrysanthemum** - Leafy greens with distinctive smell
- **Sweet potato leaves** - Heart-shaped leaves on trailing vines
- **Bitter melon** - Vine with lobed leaves, warty fruit

**Rule: If in doubt, leave it!** Ask Michael D or the plant-knowledgeable volunteer before removing anything unfamiliar.

#### Tools

- Row flattening boards (for crimping dead crops)
- Hand shears
- Scissors
- Short sickle weeders
- Knives

**Safety warning:** Sickles and knives are dangerous tools. Use with great care. Keep blades pointed down/away. Be aware of people nearby.

#### Workflow

1. **Identify keepers first** - Walk the area and flag/mark plants to keep
2. **Remove dead/spent plants** - Basil, chard, and other past-maturity crops
3. **Weed thoroughly** - Remove grass, palm seedlings, unwanted plants
4. **Flatten with boards** - Use crimping boards on dead material
5. **Mulch cleared areas** - Once weeded and flattened, apply 4" mulch
6. **Pull drip lines through** - Drip lines can stay in place, just pull up through the mulch

#### Special: Seedy Grasses

**For grasses that have gone to seed:**

1. Cut seed heads FIRST - into a plastic bag
2. Seal the bag
3. THEN remove the grass stalks
4. Dispose of seed heads in trash (NOT compost)

This prevents spreading grass seed throughout the garden.

#### Harvest for Friends

Harvest remaining edible plants to distribute:

- Chard
- Mint (relocate roots to near fruit trees)
- Kale
- Mustard greens

Bag harvested greens and set aside for distribution at the end of the day.
